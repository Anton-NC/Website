Chuseok

Chuseok is a three-day holiday that occurs around the 15th day of the 8 month every year according to the lunar calendar. It was traditionally celebrated for the purpose of gathering friends and family together to watch the full harvest moon. It is a way for families to celebrate and show gratitude to their ancestors for the fruitful harvest.

Seollal

Seollal is Korea's new years celebration. This is the time when Koreans visit their hometowns to meet up with friends and family and exchange gifts which Occurs on the first day of the lunar calendar and lasts within the span of a few days. During this holiday they execute Korean rituals, eat traditional food,play folk games and many other activities.

Dano Festival

Dano festival is one of the oldest known official Festivals in North Korea. It is Originally believed to be a spiritual ritual that was executed for the sky deity, where they celebrated the end of the sowing season. Today the dano festival is traditionally celebrated by playing folk games. Some of the games that are customarily played by women are jumping seesaw, where two women on each end of a plank supported in the middle jump up and down in order to send the opposite person flying, and Swinging, a contest where the person who swings higher wins. The men customarily played ssirum (Korean wrestling) where two contestants hold each other's thighs and attempt to throw the opponent to the ground. Traditionally the ones who came first place had the chance of winning a bull with a golden bell.
